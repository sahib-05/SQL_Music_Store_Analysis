# SQL Music Store Analysis

##  Overview

This project focuses on analyzing a Music Store database using SQL to extract meaningful business insights related to customers, sales, employees, invoices, tracks, artists, and genres.

The objective is to solve real-world business problems using SQL queries and improve data analysis skills by working with a structured relational database.

---

##  Tools & Technologies

* PostgreSQL
* SQL
* pgAdmin
* Music Store Relational Database

---

##  Key Analysis Performed

* Customer purchase behavior analysis
* Best-selling artists and tracks identification
* Revenue analysis by country and city
* Top customers by total spending
* Most popular music genres
* Employee hierarchy and senior-most employee analysis
* Invoice and sales trend analysis
* Country-wise customer distribution

---

##  Database Tables Used

The project includes analysis from tables such as:

* Customer
* Employee
* Invoice
* Invoice_Line
* Track
* Album
* Artist
* Genre
* Playlist
* Media_Type

---

##  Sample Business Questions Solved

* Who is the senior most employee based on job title?
* Which countries have the most invoices?
* What are the top 3 values of total invoice?
* Which city has the best customers based on revenue?
* Who is the best customer?
* Which genre of music is most popular?
* Which artist has earned the most revenue?

---

##  Key Insights

* Certain countries contribute significantly more revenue than others
* Rock and Pop genres dominate customer preferences
* A small group of customers generates a large share of total sales
* Some cities consistently show stronger purchasing behavior
* Top artists contribute heavily to total revenue generation

---

##  Conclusion

This project demonstrates how SQL can be used to solve business problems and generate insights from structured data. It helps strengthen query writing, joins, aggregations, subqueries, CTEs, and analytical thinking.
